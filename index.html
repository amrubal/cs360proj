About The Project

¡Bees!

Goal of the project

This project will be able to communicate the rate at which bees are dying and what is contributing to their demise through easy-to-read charts and novel ways to engage the reader/viewer to care more about not just bees, but the well-being of our planet.

Motivation

The fact that our planet is dying is no longer a faraway threat. Countless ecosystems and necessary life on our planet has been destroyed due to humanity’s need for more. Bee’s are currently dying at an alarming rate and both our economy and ecosystem are not prepared for a world without bees. I want to bring attention to an issue that has been known for a while, but it is hard to visualize the scale at which bee colonies are dying and why.

Bee Colony Numbers Across the US

US Heat Map for bee colony numbers.
2015 2016 2017 2018 2019 2020 2021

This chart is a heat map of bee colony populations across the US. This map allows for users to easily distinguish states with high colony populations from states with low colony populations. You can hover over any state and it will show the bee colony population for that state in the selected year.


Parallel Coordinate Chart

Chart for bee colony population, colony loss, number of added colonies, and number of renovated colonies.
2015 2016 2017 2018 2019 2020 2021

This visualization is a parallel coordinate chart that presents the bee colony population, colonies lost, colonies added, and the amount of colonies renovated. The states are seperated by the region they belong to. You can hover over a line and it will focus on the lines that belong to the same region. This way you can compare regions and see which regions are doing their part to save the bees.


Bee Colony losses and additions compared

Line chart to represent the change in bee colony losses and additions from 2015-2021

This line chart represents the amount of colonies added across the US. Note that data for 2019 excludes months April-June and data for 2021 was only collected for months January-June. This chart is useful as it presents the effort states across the US have made to increase their colony population.


This line chart represents the amount of colonies lost across the US. Note that data for 2019 excludes months April-June and data for 2021 was only collected for months January-June. This chart is useful as it presents the huge loss of colonies states across the US have faced in their colony population. When comparing it to the chart that represents the number of colonies added, you can ask yourself, is the US making up the deficit?


Most Impactful Bee Colony Stressors

Beeswarm for the effectiveness of bee colony stressors
2015 2016 2017 2018 2019 2020 2021

This chart is a beeswarm that presents the percent of colonies affected by a given stressor. A colony can be affected by multiple stressors at once and the values range 0.01 and up to 100. The legend contains the 6 different stressor types that have affected colonies across the US. You can hover over a dot to view the value of the stressor.
